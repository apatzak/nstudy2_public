nStudy help page

What is nStudy?

nStudy is an online learning tool. It helps you organize information about what you study and manage that information. It also keeps track of your work. In this description, we italicize terms that make up the vocabulary of nStudy and how it works.

Tip: A triangle pointing to the right means the item is folded up. Anything included in the item is hidden.A triangle pointing downwards means the item is unfolded. You can see what is included in it.




How do I get into nStudy?
  1. Open the Firefox web browser.
  2. In the address field, enter this URL: nstudy.educ.sfu.ca/dev.
  3. A message will appear asking you to install the nStudy add-on. Be sure you follow the steps to install.
  4. Create an account in nStudy.
  5. Log in using your ID and password.

When you log in, the nStudy side panel appears on the left side of your browser window. To adjust nstudy panel, click and drag the line right or left.




What to do if I forget my nStudy user name/password?
  1. Click "Forgot my password" on the login page
  2. A link to reset your password will be sent to you.
How do I create an Object?
  1. Mod-click "My folder"
    2. A mod-click is a right-click for Windows users and a control-click for Mac users

  2. Choose an object from the popup menu
    3. Available objects are: Note, Term, Document, and Folder

OR

You can also create objects by mod-clicking a collection such as:
A mod-click is a right-click for Windows users and a control-click for Mac users
  1. "See Also" in "Note" and "Term"


  2. "Notes" in Quote


  3. A Folder's title

How do I delete an Object?
  1. Mod-click it then choose Remove from the popup menu.
    2. A mod-click is a right-click for Windows users and a control-click for Mac users

How do I create and fill in a Note?
  1. Mod-click a collection.
    nStudy Collections are: "My Folder", "Folder", "See also", and "Notes" in "Bookmarks" and "Quotes"
    2. A mod-click is a right-click for Windows users and a control-click for Mac users


  2. Choose "Note" from the popup menu

  3. Give your note a title by typing over "untitled note..."


  4. Enter text by typing your text over "Enter text here..."

How do I create other objects connected to my Note?
  1. mod-click "See also", then choose from the popup menu.
    2. A mod-click is a right-click for Windows users and a control-click for Mac users


  2. if you need to label your Note as "important" or "to be reviewed..." etc., use the tag option by typing over tag


How do I copy and paste my Note?
  1. To copy a Note, mod-click it, then choose "Copy Note" from the popup menu.
    2. A mod-click is a right-click for Windows users and a control-click for Mac users


  2. To paste a Note, mod-click the collection you want to paste to, then choose "Paste Note Here" from the popup menu
    You can only paste obejcts in collections. nStudy Collections are: "My Folder", "Folder", "See also", and "Notes" in "Bookmarks" and "Quotes"


How do I write an essay?
  1. Mod-click a collection, then choose "Document"
    A mod-click is a right-click for Windows users and a control-click for Mac users
    nStudy Collections are:" My Folder", "Folder", "See also", and "Notes" in "Bookmarks" and "Quotes"

  2. Type over "untitled document" to give your essay a title

  3. Mod-click the essay's title and choose "Open in a New Window", then a popup window will appear.

  4. Write your essay in the space provided

    5. Tip: The writing document has features similar to MS Word Editor. Some of these features:
    1. Insert a table
    2. Insert an Image

How do I copy and paste in my essay?
  1. You can copy and paste Notes, Quotes, and Terms in your document. Mod-click the item you want to copy then choose "Copy" from the popup window.
    2. A mod-click is a right-click for Windows users and a control-click for Mac users

How do I drop and drag items into my essay?
  1. Mouse over the dot or triangle that marks an item
  2. Click and hold down the mouse to grab the item
  3. Drag the item to your essay
  4. Drop the item by releasing the mouse
    You can drag and drop Note, Term, Quote, Tag, "Text" in Note and "Definition" in Term
