How to get started?
There are two ways to get started:
  1. Save a bookmark
  2. Create an object using My Folders
    3. NOTE: nStudy objects are: Note, Term, Document, and Folder
How do I save a bookmark?
In nStudy, a bookmark refers to a web page you have visited on the Internet.
  1. Mod-click anywhere in the webpage you opened
    2. NOTE: A mod-click is a right-click for Windows users and a control-click for Mac users

  2. Choose “Bookmark Page on nStudy” from the pop up menu
    3. NOTE: When you quote a text on a bookmark, the webpage is automatically saved under Recent Bookmarks


  3. The bookmark will appear under Recent Bookmarks and Everything on nStudy panel
    4. NOTE: bookmarks are listed by time, most recent on top



  4. You can rename the webpage title by typing over it


  5. You can copy a bookmark by mod-clicking it and choosing “Copy Bookmark” from the popup menu


  6. Then you can paste it in: “Folder” , “See Also” in “Terms” and “Note”, and “Notes” in “Annotate and Quote” and under a bookmark


Tip:To revisit a bookmark double click the bookmark title saved in Recent Bookmarks OR mod-click the bookmark and choose “Go to bookmark” from the popup menu
How to create and fill in a term?
  1. Mod-click a collection
    2. NOTE: nStudy collections are: “My Folder”, “Folder”, “See also”, and “Notes” in “Bookmarks” and “Quotes”
  2. Choose “Term” from the popup menu


  3. Type over “untitled term”to write your term


  4. Then click on the triangle pointing to the right
  5. The Term will unfold
  6. Type in the definition of your term over “Enter text here. . . ”


  7. To create other objects connected to your Term, mod-click “See also”
  8. Then choose an object from the popup menu


  9. If you need to label your term as “ important ” or “to be reviewed ” . . . etc ., use the tag option by typing over tag


  10. To copy a term, mod-click it, then choose “Copy Term” from the popup menu


  11. To paste a term, mod-click the collection you want to paste to, then choose “Paste Term Here ” from the popup menu


Tip: “Terms Used”lists all the terms that appear in that webpage or Term.


How to add a quote?
If you highlight text in the browser, you can quote that text in the nStudy panel. To add a quote :
  1. Access any webpage
  2. Select text in the browser panel you want to quote
  3. Mod-click anywhere in the text you selected


  4. In the popup menu, choose Quote
  5. The quote will appear in the nStudy panel

    6. Tip:You can choose “Quote & Annotate” if you want to add objects to your quote


Tip:You can also add objects to Quotes by unfolding the quote , mod-clicking “Notes” and choosing an object from the pop up menu


How to search on nStudy?
nStudy allows you to search for items you created. To search for an item:
  1. Go to the search area at the top of the nStudy panel
  2. Type in the key word you are looking for over “enter search here”


  3. All objects with the key word will appear under the search area.
